The following poems are about school shootings and/or mass shootings. Please read at your own discretion



Another Monday

I stumble through the school,
talking to my friends.
A normal Monday morning,
with pencils and pens.

A loud noise fills the air,
classmates turn frightened.
Shouting begins,
my heart rate is heightened.

My feet begin running,
with my mind still frozen,
as I hear a student ask,
“Why was this school chosen?”

The fireworks come closer,
I pull on doors to hide.
But teachers have a duty,
to protect those locked inside.

Brain fires thoughts,
where to go, where to run?
I find I’m out of options,
and fear my death has begun.

I get a slight chill,
from an ear-piercing scream.
Red flows around the corner,
like water from a stream.

Footsteps approach me,
a mask in front of my face.
As the barrel meets my eye,
I see my darkened bookcase.

I sit up in bed,
my eyes still adjusting.
“It was just a dream,”
I say, yet untrusting.

But that’s just it,
this is no nightmare.
It may not be here,
but it’s reality is elsewhere.

A school close by,
a school far away.
Different students will face,
another normal Monday.



To Hear and To See

First I hear a loud bang;
I see a kid fall.
Surely he’s just tripped
and slammed into the wall.

And then I hear the screams,
then I see the red,
as the horizontal rain,
tears flesh like it’s thread.

That's when I hear the running;
when I see the still figures.
Is this what they feel,
the bodies in the pictures?

The people I hear on T.V.,
people I see online,
the others who have faced,
this experience of mine.

Now I hear the breathing,
I see the heavy boots.
I’m alone in the hall now,
with the person who shoots.



Another

Another day,
Another mention.
Something old,
creates more tension.

Another face,
Hidden in the sky.
Another grave,
Left for to die.

Another school,
Another piece of news.
Another terror,
Left behind bloodied shoes.

Another loud noise
Another long pause.
Loud screaming,
Fills all the halls.

Another body,
Thuds as it hits the floor.
Another person,
Behind a locker door,

Another shooting,
Gone goes a friend.
Another tragedy,
Where is the end?



If I Die

If I die,
Post it everywhere,
Use my name as a war cry
To demand better.
If I die,
Remember it’s your fault
Racism was more important
Than bringing bullets to a halt.
If I die,
Let this be a symbol
That your negligence,
Has inspired art as a whole.
If I die,
Of another white supremacist,
Don’t tell me I’M the nazi
For being a feminist.
If I die,
At a concert or in class,
Save your thoughts and prayers
For the rest of the mass.
Because if I die,
I can no longer fight
For the good not the rich,
As I’ve now entered the light.
