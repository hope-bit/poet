Rabbit Hole

I fell down a hole
With no rabbit in sight.
No fairy tale ending,
Or wishes by night.

This house that I’ve dug
Is more than to hide.
I’m stuck by myself
With ghosts by my side.

Temporary life
Fits a temporary living.
This pit where I lay,
the gift that keeps giving.



Asleep

I’m falling asleep.

And not the kind of sleep
Where one drifts off to a happy place,
But one were things are dark and tiring.
Filling the void with more empty spaces.

I’m falling asleep.

I’m beginning to lose all focus,
Trying to keep my mind awake and sound,
But things are getting heavier
Forcing me to drop towards the ground.

Thing’s don’t process.

It’s like a worn-out train,
Needs to be fixed but no one’s around.
I wonder if there were warning signs,
Signs allowing me to escape from the bound.

It’s creating distance,

Distance away from other people,
Away from family and surrounding peers.
Is everything in check?
All the wires in place, with well-working gears?

I’m falling asleep.

But to sleep is to be open to everything,
It is where all the feelings rain in.
Drowning everything else until it’s gone,
Making me feel guilty even where there is no sin,
And pondering what stars I’ve been wishing upon.

I’m falling asleep.
I’m falling asleep.

I dread the day that I no longer fear it.
The day in which I’ve succumbed to it all.
I may be small in stature,
But I want to remain standing metaphorically tall.



A Friend's Betrayal

A friend’s betrayal
Hurts more than a love’s.
For you give your heart to your love,
But your entirety to the friend.
